2026-07-21 Droppings and spiders

Spending more time outside, I feel as though I am learning all the time. This year, in two separate anchorages, paper wasps came aboard to try and build nests in our folded mainsail. The mainsail is covered by a cover, providing plenty of shelter from the elements. I did not know that paper wasps liked to build little mud nests in such places, and was also shocked to find out they fill these nests with spiders. Yes. The paper wasps stun, or kill(I'm not sure) many spiders and stash them in our mainsail. Because we were able to deter them often enough before they could finish they never did make babies here, but on both occasions they had time to deposit 10-20 spiders. When raising the mainsail, the mud nests and spiders would all come falling out on deck. A terrible sight. I am not afraid of spiders, but it is difficult to not react to a pile of spiders. And so one lesson I learned this summer was that paper wasps are very persistent.
Another lesson, was that I learned to recognize wolf poop after seeing some for the first time on a trail on Cortes Island. There is a resident pack of black and brindled wolves there, but apparently transient packs swim in from neighbouring islands. Wolf poop has a lot of hair in it, as well as bone fragments. Since then, I've been reading up on wildlife poop in general to learn to recognize them when out on hikes. At one point I had 5 tabs open, all about animal poop. Bear poop, deer poop, wolf poop, cougar poop... poop poop poop.

READING. I am currently reading Katherine Addison's The Goblin Emperor aloud to Devine every night. It is very fun to read, although the names in the book are a bit difficult to manage, especially since titles of address further add to the confusion. I do love though that the author decided to go all in on this, to not compromise. This is not a very exciting book, but it is nice to dive into her world, to learn of its characters, politics, formalities of dress, etc. One point I especially love is the special attention given to the ears of the characters, used to describe their emotional state. Sometimes the characters have to make a conscious effort to control their ears positions to not let others know that they are fearful, or angry. I love it because it is one of the main reasons why the characters in {Hakum} have such ears, it allows for a much larger range of expression.
